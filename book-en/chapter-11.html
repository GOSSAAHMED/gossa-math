11.1 Overview

With the formal definition established, we now derive the general laws governing hierarchical derivatives and show that they arise naturally from the scale structure itself.

11.2 General Definition

\[ D_n y=\frac{d(L_n(y))}{d(L_n(x))},\quad L_n(t)=\ln^{(n)}(t) \]

11.3 Invariance under Scaling

For any constant \(c>0\), \[ D_n(cy)=D_n(y),\quad n\ge1. \]

11.4 Product Behavior

Only at level \(D_1\) does multiplication transform into addition. Higher levels encode product structure differently.

11.5 Transition Formula

\[ D_{n+1}y=\frac{L_n(x)}{L_n(y)}\,D_n y \]

11.6 Power Laws

If \(y=x^a\) with \(x>1\), then \(D_1y=a\) and \(D_ny=1\) for all \(n\ge2\).

11.7 Functional Classification

  • Power laws: rapid hierarchical stabilization.
  • Exponential laws: gradual scale transition.
  • Composite laws: multi-scale structure.

11.8 Summary

  • Hierarchical laws follow from scale structure.
  • The transition formula is central.
  • Next chapters apply these laws to approximation and equations.
