Chapter 11 — Higher-Order Hierarchical Derivatives \(D_n\)

Generalization, hierarchy, and scale stability

11.1 From \(D_1\) and \(D_2\) to \(D_n\)

The logarithmic construction naturally extends to arbitrary depth, leading to a full hierarchy of derivatives.

11.2 General Definition

Let \[ L_0(t)=t,\quad L_1(t)=\ln t,\quad L_2(t)=\ln\ln t,\dots \] Then: \[ D_n y=\frac{d(L_n(y))}{d(L_n(x))}. \]

11.3 General Formula

\[ D_n y = \frac{x\prod_{k=1}^{n-1}\ln^{(k)}x} {y\prod_{k=1}^{n-1}\ln^{(k)}y} \;\frac{dy}{dx}. \]

11.4 Transition Between Levels

\[ D_{n+1}y=\frac{\ln^{(n)}x}{\ln^{(n)}y}\,D_n y. \]

11.5 Power Laws

For \(y=Cx^a\): \[ D_1y=a,\qquad D_ny=1\ (n\ge2). \]

11.6 Examples

\(y=x^2:\; D_1=2,\; D_2=1,\; D_3=1.\)
\(y=e^x:\; D_1=x,\; D_2=\ln x,\; D_3=\ln\ln x.\)
\(y=e^{e^x}:\; D_1=e^x,\; D_2=x,\; D_3=\ln x.\)

11.7 Interpretation

  • Each level probes a deeper scale.
  • Power laws are fixed points of the hierarchy.
  • \(D_n\) captures multi-scale growth.

11.8 Summary

  • Hierarchical derivatives form a structured sequence.
  • Higher levels reveal deeper invariance.
  • This framework enables new approximation methods.
