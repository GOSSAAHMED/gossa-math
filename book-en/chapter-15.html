Chapter 15 — Integration in Hierarchical Calculus

Reconstructing functions from hierarchical derivatives

15.1 Conceptual Introduction

In hierarchical calculus, integration reconstructs a function from its relative or logarithmic structure, rather than from absolute slopes.

15.2 Relative Integration (Inverse of \(D_1\))

If \[ D_1y=f(x), \] then \[ \ln y=\int f(x)\,d(\ln x)+C. \]

15.3 Ten Numerical Examples

Example 1: \(D_1y=2 \Rightarrow y=Cx^2\)
Example 2: \(D_1y=-1 \Rightarrow y=C/x\)
Example 3: \(D_1y=x \Rightarrow y=Ce^x\)
Example 4: \(D_1y=\ln x \Rightarrow y=\exp((\ln x)^2/2)\)
Example 5: \(D_1y=a+b/\ln x \Rightarrow y=Cx^a(\ln x)^b\)
Example 6: \(D_1y=3x^2 \Rightarrow y=Ce^{x^3}\)
Example 7: \(D_1y=1/\ln x \Rightarrow y=C\ln x\)
Example 8: \(D_1y=\ln\ln x \Rightarrow y=\exp(\int\ln\ln x\,d\ln x)\)
Example 9: \(D_1y=x^n \Rightarrow y=Ce^{x^{n+1}/(n+1)}\)
Example 10: \(D_1y=f(\ln x)\Rightarrow y=\exp(\int f(u)\,du)\)

15.4 Logarithmic Integration (Inverse of \(D_2\))

\[ \ln\ln y=\int g(x)\,d(\ln\ln x)+C. \]

15.5 Interpretation

  • Integration rebuilds hierarchical structure.
  • Relative integrals are simpler than classical ones.
  • Deep growth laws become explicit.

15.6 Summary

  • Hierarchical integration is the natural inverse of \(D_n\).
  • Ten examples demonstrate its power.
  • This opens the door to hierarchical differential equations.
