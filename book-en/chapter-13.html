13.1 Core Idea

Many equations look complicated in the raw variable \(x\) because the natural physics lives on a different scale (e.g., \(\ln x\), \(\ln\ln x\), ...). Hierarchical calculus changes the scale so that the equation becomes linear or transparent.

13.2 Transformation Dictionary

Level \(D_1\): \[ D_1y=\frac{d(\ln y)}{d(\ln x)}=\frac{x}{y}\frac{dy}{dx} \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{y}{x}D_1y. \]
Level \(D_2\): \[ D_2y=\frac{d(\ln\ln y)}{d(\ln\ln x)} =\frac{x\ln x}{y\ln y}\frac{dy}{dx} \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{y\ln y}{x\ln x}D_2y. \]
Level \(D_3\) (when needed): \[ D_3y=\frac{d(\ln\ln\ln y)}{d(\ln\ln\ln x)}. \]

13.3 Seven Numerical Examples

Each example shows: a heavy/nonlinear \(D_0\) form, a simplified hierarchical form, the closed solution, and a numerical check.

Example 1 ‚Äî Variable coefficient becomes constant (Level \(D_1\))

\(D_0\): \(\; y'=\frac{2}{x}y\).

\(D_1\): \(\; D_1y=2\).

Solution: \(y=Cx^2\). With \(y(2)=8\Rightarrow y(5)=50\).

Example 2 ‚Äî Strong nonlinearity becomes linear in \(w=\ln y\) (Level \(D_1\))

\(D_0\): \(\; y'=\frac{y}{x}\cdot \frac12\ln y\).

\(D_1\): \(\; D_1y=\frac12\ln y\). Let \(w=\ln y\), \(u=\ln x\): \(dw/du=\frac12 w\).

Solution: with \(y(1)=e\): \(y=\exp(\sqrt{x})\). Check: \(y(9)=e^3=20.0855369\).

Example 3 ‚Äî \(D_2y=3\) is linear on the \(\ln\ln\) scale

\(D_2\): \(\; D_2y=3\Rightarrow \ln\ln y=3\ln\ln x +C\).

Solution (choose \(C=0\)): \(y=\exp((\ln x)^3)\). Check: \(y(e^3)=e^{27}=5.320482406\times 10^{11}\).

Example 4 ‚Äî \(\ln\ln x\)-forcing integrates directly (Level \(D_2\))

\(D_2\): \(\; D_2y = 1+\frac{1}{\ln\ln x}\).

Let \(v=\ln\ln x\): \(d(\ln\ln y)/dv = 1+1/v\).

Solution (choose \(C=0\)): \(y=\exp((\ln x)(\ln\ln x))\). Check: \(y(e^5)=5^5=3125\).

Example 5 ‚Äî Linear ODE in \(w=\ln y\) (Level \(D_1\))

\(D_1\): \(\; D_1y = 1+2\ln y\).

Let \(w=\ln y\), \(u=\ln x\): \(dw/du = 1+2w\) (linear).

Solution with \(y(1)=1\): \(y=\exp\!\left(\frac12(x^2-1)\right)\). Check: \(y(3)=e^4=54.5981500\).

Example 6 ‚Äî Fully linear on \(z=\ln\ln y\) (Level \(D_2\))

\(D_2\): \(\; D_2y = 2+\frac12\ln\ln y\).

Let \(z=\ln\ln y\), \(v=\ln\ln x\): \(dz/dv = 2+\frac12 z\) (linear).

With \(x_0=e^2,\, y_0=e\): at \(x=e^5\): \(y\approx 27505.28646\).

Example 7 ‚Äî Level \(D_3\): a deep-scale linearization

\(D_3\): \(D_3y=1\Rightarrow \ln\ln\ln y = \ln\ln\ln x + C\).

Solution: \(y=\exp((\ln x)^B)\). Choose \(B=2\): \(y=\exp((\ln x)^2)\).

Check: \(y(e^3)=e^9=8103.0839276\).

13.4 Summary

  • Scale choice can be the difference between ‚Äúheavy nonlinear‚Äù and ‚Äúlinear solvable‚Äù.
  • \(D_1\) often linearizes equations in \(\ln y\) vs \(\ln x\).
  • \(D_2\) and \(D_3\) directly handle nested-log structures.
