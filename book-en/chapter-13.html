Chapter 13 — Relations Between Levels of the Derivative Hierarchy

Transition laws, stability, and extensive numerical examples

13.1 Overview

This chapter analyzes how hierarchical derivatives relate across levels, revealing a structured transition from \(D_0\) to arbitrarily high \(D_n\).

13.2 Fundamental Transition Relation

\[ D_{n+1}y=\frac{L_n(x)}{L_n(y)}\,D_ny. \]

13.3 Relation to the Classical Derivative

\[ D_n y= \frac{x\prod_{k=1}^{n-1}L_k(x)} {y\prod_{k=1}^{n-1}L_k(y)}\,\frac{dy}{dx}. \]

13.4 Hierarchical Stability

A function is hierarchically stable from level \(n_0\) if \(D_ny\) is constant for all \(n\ge n_0\).

13.5 Extensive Numerical Examples

Example A: Power law \(y=x^2\)

\(D_1=2,\; D_n=1\ (n\ge2)\).

Example B: Exponential \(y=e^x\)

\(D_1=x,\; D_2=\ln x,\; D_3=\ln\ln x,\; D_4=\ln\ln\ln x\).

Example C: Double exponential \(y=e^{e^x}\)

\(D_1=e^x,\; D_2=x,\; D_3=\ln x,\; D_4=\ln\ln x\).

Example D: Polynomial with shift \(y=x^2+1\)

\(D_2\to1,\; D_3\to1\) as \(x\to\infty\).

Example E: Composite growth \(y=x^a(\ln x)^b\)

\(D_1=a+\frac{b}{\ln x},\; D_2\to1\).

13.6 Interpretation

  • Each level removes one layer of complexity.
  • Stability indicates intrinsic structural simplicity.
  • Complex growth simplifies at the correct scale.

13.7 Summary

  • Hierarchical relations connect all derivative levels.
  • Power laws are fixed points.
  • Higher derivatives reveal deep invariance.
