Chapter 33 — Data, Ratios, and Logarithmic Scales

From raw values to hierarchical information

Why Data Is Relative

In data science, absolute values rarely matter. Ratios and logarithmic scales reveal structure.

Relative Derivatives

\[ D_1y=\frac{d(\ln y)}{d(\ln x)} \] captures relative growth.

Examples

Zipf: \(f(r)\sim r^{-1}\)
Power law: \(P(x)\sim x^{-\gamma}\)
Growth: \(y\sim x^{1.5}\)
Log-loss: \(L\sim \ln(1+e)\)

Summary

  • Data lives on logarithmic scales.
  • Ratios reveal structure.
  • Hierarchical calculus unifies data analysis.
