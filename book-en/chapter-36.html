Chapter 36 — Limits of the Theory and Ethics of Use

Between explanatory power and scientific responsibility

Why Discuss Limits?

Every powerful framework carries risks of misuse. Recognizing limits is a mark of maturity.

Mathematical Limits

  • Not all functions admit effective hierarchical descriptions.
  • Logarithmic transforms require positive domains.
  • Noise can obscure hierarchical structure.

Modeling Limits

  • Hierarchy does not replace good data.
  • Effective models are scale-dependent.
  • Transitions may require hybrid approaches.

Ethical Considerations

Prediction vs Understanding

  • Hierarchical calculus favors understanding.
  • Long-term prediction is inherently limited.

Responsible Use Principles

  • Start from the lowest adequate level.
  • Validate with data.
  • Declare assumptions and limits.

Final Remarks

Science without ethics is dangerous; ethics without science is powerless.
