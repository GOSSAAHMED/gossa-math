Chapter 12 — General Definition of the Hierarchical Derivative \(D_n\)

Unified formulation, domain conditions, and transition relations

12.1 Motivation

After introducing low-order logarithmic derivatives, we now formalize a general definition encompassing all hierarchical levels.

12.2 Iterated Logarithmic Functions

\[ L_0(t)=t,\quad L_1(t)=\ln t,\quad L_2(t)=\ln\ln t,\ \dots \] \[ L_n(t)=\ln^{(n)}(t). \]

12.3 General Definition

\[ D_n y=\frac{d(L_n(y))}{d(L_n(x))},\qquad n=0,1,2,\dots \]

12.4 Domain Conditions

\[ x>\exp^{(n-1)}(1),\qquad y>\exp^{(n-1)}(1). \]

12.5 General Formula

\[ D_n y = \frac{x\prod_{k=1}^{n-1}L_k(x)} {y\prod_{k=1}^{n-1}L_k(y)} \frac{dy}{dx}. \]

12.6 Transition Relation

\[ D_{n+1}y=\frac{L_n(x)}{L_n(y)}\,D_ny. \]

12.7 Examples

\(y=x^2:\; D_1=2,\; D_2=1,\; D_3=1.\)
\(y=e^x:\; D_1=x,\; D_2=\ln x,\; D_3=\ln\ln x.\)
\(y=e^{e^x}:\; D_1=e^x,\; D_2=x,\; D_3=\ln x.\)

12.8 Interpretation

  • \(D_0\): absolute change
  • \(D_1\): relative change
  • \(D_2\): change of relative change
  • \(D_n\): deeper hierarchical change

12.9 Summary

  • A unified definition covers all hierarchical derivatives.
  • Transition relations link consecutive levels.
  • Power laws are fixed points of the hierarchy.
  • This framework enables hierarchical series and equations.
