12.1 Core Idea

Classical Taylor (the \(D_0\) expansion) develops around \(x_0\) in the variable \(x\):

\[ f(x)\approx \sum_{k=0}^{N}\frac{f^{(k)}(x_0)}{k!}(x-x_0)^k. \]

Hierarchical calculus proposes developing the same function on a deeper scale:

  • \(D_1\)-scale: \(u=\ln x\)
  • \(D_2\)-scale: \(v=\ln\ln x\)
  • In general: \(L_n(x)=\ln^{(n)}(x)\)
Often, \( |\ln(x)-\ln(x_0)| \) is much smaller than \( |x-x_0| \), and similarly \( |\ln\ln(x)-\ln\ln(x_0)| \) can be even smaller on very large ranges. Smaller expansion variables typically mean faster convergence for the same truncation order.

12.2 The \(D_1\) Super-Differential Series

Let \(u=\ln x\) and \(F(u)=f(e^u)\). Expand at \(u_0=\ln x_0\):

\[ f(x)=F(u)\approx \sum_{k=0}^{N}\frac{F^{(k)}(u_0)}{k!}\,(u-u_0)^k, \quad u=\ln x. \]

12.3 The \(D_2\) Super-Differential Series

Let \(v=\ln\ln x\) and \(G(v)=f(e^{e^v})\). Expand at \(v_0=\ln\ln x_0\):

\[ f(x)=G(v)\approx \sum_{k=0}^{N}\frac{G^{(k)}(v_0)}{k!}\,(v-v_0)^k, \quad v=\ln\ln x. \]
