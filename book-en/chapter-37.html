Chapter 37 — Hierarchical Calculus vs Classical Calculus

From local change to multi-level dynamics

Conceptual Overview

Classical calculus measures local absolute change. Hierarchical calculus extends this view to relative and multi-scale change.

What Each Measures

MethodWhat it captures
Classical calculusLocal rate of change
Relative derivative \(D_1\)Relative growth
Hierarchical calculusScale transitions

Example: Exponential Growth

  • \(D_0y=e^x\)
  • \(D_1y=1\)
  • \(D_2y=0\)

Handling Nonlinearity

ProblemClassicalHierarchical
ExponentialGrowingStable
Super-exponentialBlow-upStable at higher level

When to Use Each

  • Classical: precise local dynamics.
  • Hierarchical: scaling and structure.

Conclusion

Hierarchical calculus complements, not replaces, classical calculus.
