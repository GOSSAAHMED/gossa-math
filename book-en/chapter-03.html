3.1 Absolute Change

Absolute change measures variation without reference to the size of the quantity itself. In classical calculus it is expressed as:

\[ D_0 y = \frac{dy}{dx} \]

This measure is powerful but blind to scale effects.

3.2 Relative Change

Relative change compares variation to the magnitude of the quantity:

\[ \frac{dy}{y} \]

This captures proportional significance rather than raw difference.

3.3 Conceptual Difference

Two systems may exhibit the same absolute change, yet behave very differently when viewed relatively.

3.4 Toward the Relative Derivative

Comparing relative changes in \(y\) and \(x\) leads naturally to:

\[ \frac{dy/y}{dx/x} \]

This expression underlies the definition of the relative derivative \(D_1\).
