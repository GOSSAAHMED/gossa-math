Chapter 16 — Relative Integration

Reconstructing functions from relative growth laws

16.1 Conceptual Meaning

Relative integration reconstructs a function from its relative rate of change, rather than absolute slope.

16.2 Formal Definition

If \[ D_1y=f(x), \] then \[ \ln y=\int f(x)\,d(\ln x)+C. \]

16.3 Why Relative Integration Is Simpler

  • Direct handling of power laws.
  • Multiplicative structures become additive.
  • Nonlinear ODEs reduce to simple integrals.

16.4 Twelve Numerical Examples

1. \(D_1y=2 \Rightarrow y=Cx^2\)
2. \(D_1y=-1 \Rightarrow y=C/x\)
3. \(D_1y=x \Rightarrow y=Ce^x\)
4. \(D_1y=\ln x \Rightarrow y=\exp((\ln x)^2/2)\)
5. \(D_1y=a+b/\ln x \Rightarrow y=Cx^a(\ln x)^b\)
6. \(D_1y=3x^2 \Rightarrow y=Ce^{x^3}\)
7. \(D_1y=1/\ln x \Rightarrow y=C\ln x\)
8. \(D_1y=\ln\ln x \Rightarrow y=\exp(\int\ln\ln x\,d\ln x)\)
9. \(D_1y=x^n \Rightarrow y=Ce^{x^{n+1}/(n+1)}\)
10. \(D_1y=\sin(\ln x) \Rightarrow y=e^{-\cos(\ln x)}\)
11. \(D_1y=\ln(x^2+1)\)
12. \(D_1y=f(\ln x)\Rightarrow y=\exp(\int f(u)\,du)\)

16.5 Geometric Interpretation

  • Integration occurs in log–log space.
  • Rebuilds curves from scale-based slopes.
  • Solutions are structurally meaningful.

16.6 Summary

  • Relative integration is the inverse of \(D_1\).
  • It simplifies complex differential equations.
  • It is central to hierarchical calculus.
