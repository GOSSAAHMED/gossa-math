16.1 A Practical Workflow

  1. Write the empirical/physical law as given.
  2. Detect structure: \(y'/y \Rightarrow D_1\), \(y'/(y\ln y)\Rightarrow D_2\), \(y'/(y\ln y\ln\ln y)\Rightarrow D_3\).
  3. Choose scale: \(u=\ln x\), \(v=\ln\ln x\), \(s=\ln\ln\ln x\).
  4. Choose transform: \(w=\ln y\), \(z=\ln\ln y\), \(q=\ln\ln\ln y\).
  5. Make it linear in \(w(u)\) or \(z(v)\) or \(q(s)\), solve, invert back.
  6. Validate numerically at a chosen point.

16.2 Quick Dictionary

\[ D_1y=\frac{x}{y}y',\qquad D_2y=\frac{x\ln x}{y\ln y}y',\qquad D_3y=\frac{x\ln x\,\ln\ln x}{y\ln y\,\ln\ln y}y'. \]

16.3 Physics Examples (6)

(F1) Similarity law: constant \(D_1y\)

\(D_1y=\alpha\Rightarrow y=Cx^\alpha\). Choose \(\alpha=2\). If \(y(3)=18\Rightarrow C=2\). Then \(y(10)=200\).

(F2) \((\ln x)^2\)-driven law becomes trivial in \(u=\ln x\)

\(D_1y=(\ln x)^2\Rightarrow \ln y=u^3/3 \Rightarrow y=\exp((\ln x)^3/3)\). Check: \(x=e^3\Rightarrow y=e^9=8103.08393\).

(F3) Pattern \(y'/(y\ln y)\Rightarrow D_2\)

\(y'=\frac{y\ln y}{x\ln x}\Rightarrow D_2y=1\Rightarrow y=x\). Check: \(x=500\Rightarrow y=500\).

(F4) Constant \(D_2y\)

\(D_2y=2\Rightarrow y=\exp((\ln x)^2)\). Check: \(x=e^3\Rightarrow y=e^9=8103.08393\).

(F5) Deep multi-scale law: constant \(D_3y\)

\(D_3y=2\Rightarrow y=\exp(\exp((\ln\ln x)^2))\). Check: \(x=e^{e^2}\Rightarrow y=\exp(\exp(4))\approx 2.51\times10^{23}\).

(F6) \(D_2y=1+\ln\ln x\) integrates in \(v=\ln\ln x\)

\(dz/dv=1+v\Rightarrow z=v+v^2/2\Rightarrow y=\exp(\exp(v+v^2/2))\). For \(x=e^{e^2}\Rightarrow v=2\Rightarrow y=\exp(\exp(4))\).

16.4 Economics / Growth Examples (5)

(E1) Constant elasticity: \(D_1y=\varepsilon\)

With \(\varepsilon=0.7\), \(y(100)=50\Rightarrow C\approx 1.9905\). Then \(y(200)\approx 81.22\).

(E2) \(D_1y=1+\ln x\Rightarrow\) closed form

\(d(\ln y)/du=1+u\Rightarrow y=x\exp((\ln x)^2/2)\). Check: \(x=e^2\Rightarrow y=e^4=54.59815\).

(E3) \(D_2y=1+\frac{1}{\ln\ln x}\Rightarrow y=5^5\) at \(x=e^5\)

\(y=\exp((\ln x)(\ln\ln x))\). Check: \(x=e^5\Rightarrow y=3125\).

(E4) Nonlinear in \(y\) becomes linear in \(w=\ln y\)

\(y'=\frac{y}{x}(2+\ln y)\Rightarrow dw/du=2+w\Rightarrow y=e^{x-2}\) with \(y(1)=e^{-1}\). Check: \(y(4)=e^2\).

(E5) Deep-scale normalization: \(D_3y=1\Rightarrow y=x\)

Choose \(C=0\Rightarrow y=x\). Check: \(x=e^{e^2}\Rightarrow y=e^{e^2}\).

16.5 Engineering / Control / Materials Examples (5)

(G1) \(D_1y=a+b\ln x\Rightarrow y=Cx^a\exp(\frac{b}{2}(\ln x)^2)\)

With \(a=b=C=1\): \(x=e^2\Rightarrow y=e^4=54.59815\).

(G2) Direct design pattern: \(y'=\frac{2y\ln y}{x\ln x}\Rightarrow D_2y=2\)

With \(y(e)=e\Rightarrow y=\exp((\ln x)^2)\). Check: \(x=e^3\Rightarrow y=e^9\).

(G3) Linear control law on \(z=\ln\ln y\): \(D_2y=3+\frac12\ln\ln y\)

Solve \(dz/dv=3+\frac12 z\). With \(x_0=e^2,y_0=e\), at \(x=e^5\): \(z\approx 3.4868\Rightarrow y=\exp(\exp(z))\sim 10^{14}\).

(G4) Choose \(D_3\) due to \(\ln\ln\ln\) forcing

\(D_3y=1+\frac{1}{\ln\ln\ln x}\Rightarrow q=s+\ln s\). If \(s=2\) (i.e., \(x=\exp(\exp(\exp(2)))\)), then \(q\approx 2.6931\Rightarrow \ln\ln y\approx 14.78\Rightarrow y=\exp(\exp(14.78))\).

(G5) Calibration from two points under a power law

\(y=Cx^\alpha\). From \(y(2)=10,y(8)=80\Rightarrow \alpha=3/2\), \(C\approx 3.5355339\). Then \(y(18)\approx 270.0\).

16.6 Practical Takeaway

  • Start from structure, not from brute-force solving.
  • Pick \(D_1/D_2/D_3\) based on the pattern in the equation.
  • Linearize in \(\ln y\), \(\ln\ln y\), \(\ln\ln\ln y\) on the matching scale.
  • Always validate numerically at at least one point.
