14.1 Why an Algorithm?

The key power of hierarchical calculus is not merely defining \(D_n\), but systematically choosing the level that turns a heavy/nonlinear \(D_0\) equation into a linear or directly integrable equation on a deeper scale.

14.2 Practical Decision Tree

  • If you see \(\frac{y'}{y}\) or \(\frac{x}{y}y'\): try \(D_1\).
  • If you see \(\frac{y'}{y\ln y}\) or \(\frac{x\ln x}{y\ln y}y'\): try \(D_2\).
  • If you see \(\frac{y'}{y\ln y\ln\ln y}\): try \(D_3\).
  • If the natural forcing depends on \(\ln x\): use \(u=\ln x\) (level \(D_1\)).
  • If it depends on \(\ln\ln x\): use \(v=\ln\ln x\) (level \(D_2\)).

14.3 Transformation Dictionary

\[ D_1y=\frac{x}{y}y' \iff y'=\frac{y}{x}D_1y. \] \[ D_2y=\frac{x\ln x}{y\ln y}y' \iff y'=\frac{y\ln y}{x\ln x}D_2y. \] \[ D_3y=\frac{x\ln x\,\ln\ln x}{y\ln y\,\ln\ln y}y'. \]

14.4 A General Design Method

  1. Inspect the equation: does it involve \(y,\ln y,\ln\ln y\) and \(x,\ln x,\ln\ln x\)?
  2. Choose the natural scale variable: \(u=\ln x\), \(v=\ln\ln x\), etc.
  3. Choose the solution transform: \(w=\ln y\), \(z=\ln\ln y\), etc.
  4. Rewrite to obtain a linear ODE in \(w(u)\) or \(z(v)\).
  5. Solve and invert back to \(y\).
  6. Validate with a numerical check.

14.5 Many Numerical Examples (12)

Example 1 (D1): \(D_1y=3\Rightarrow y=Cx^3\)

With \(y(2)=16\Rightarrow C=2\), then \(y(5)=250\).

Example 2 (D1): \(D_1y=1+\ln y\Rightarrow y=e^{x-1}\)

Using \(y(1)=1\Rightarrow y=e^{x-1}\). Check: \(y(3)=e^2=7.3890561\).

Example 3 (D1): \(D_1y=2\ln x\Rightarrow y=e^{(\ln x)^2}\)

With \(y(e)=e\Rightarrow y=e^{(\ln x)^2}\). Check: \(y(e^3)=e^9=8103.08393\).

Example 4 (D1): \(D_1y=a+b\ln x\Rightarrow y=Cx^a\exp(\tfrac{b}{2}(\ln x)^2)\)

Take \(a=b=C=1\). Check: \(y(e^2)=e^4=54.59815\).

Example 5 (D2): \(D_2y=2\Rightarrow y=\exp((\ln x)^2)\)

Check: \(y(e^3)=e^9=8103.08393\).

Example 6 (D2): \(D_2y=1+\frac{1}{\ln\ln x}\Rightarrow y=\exp((\ln x)(\ln\ln x))\)

Check: \(y(e^5)=5^5=3125\).

Example 7 (D2): \(D_2y=\ln\ln y\Rightarrow y=e^x\) (for a specific constant)

One gets \(\ln\ln y=C\ln x\Rightarrow y=e^{x^C}\). Choose \(C=1\Rightarrow y=e^x\). Check: \(y(2)=e^2\).

Example 8 (D2): \(D_2y=3+\tfrac12\ln\ln y\Rightarrow\) linear ODE in \(z=\ln\ln y\)

With \(x_0=e^2,y_0=e\), at \(x=e^5\) one gets \(z\approx 3.4868\) hence \(y\approx \exp(\exp(z))\sim 10^{14}\).

Example 9 (D1): \(y'=\frac{y}{x}(2+\ln y)\Rightarrow y=e^{x-2}\)

Using \(y(1)=e^{-1}\Rightarrow y=e^{x-2}\). Check: \(y(4)=e^2\).

Example 10 (D1): \(D_1y=(\ln x)^2\Rightarrow y=\exp((\ln x)^3/3)\)

Check: \(y(e^3)=e^9=8103.08393\).

Example 11 (D3): \(D_3y=2\Rightarrow \ln\ln y=(\ln\ln x)^2\)

At \(x=e^{e^2}\) we have \(\ln\ln x=2\), so \(y=\exp(\exp(4))\approx 2.51\times 10^{23}\).

Example 12 (Auto-choice): \(\;y'=\frac{y\ln y}{x\ln x}\Rightarrow D_2y=1\Rightarrow y=x\)

Check: \(x=100\Rightarrow y=100\).

14.6 Summary

  • Level choice is guided by structural patterns, not guesswork.
  • \(\frac{y'}{y}\to D_1\), \(\frac{y'}{y\ln y}\to D_2\), \(\frac{y'}{y\ln y\ln\ln y}\to D_3\).
  • After choosing the level, transform the dependent variable (\(\ln y\), \(\ln\ln y\)) to obtain a linear ODE.
