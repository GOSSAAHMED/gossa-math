9.1 Logarithmic Linearity

\[ D_1(y_1y_2)=D_1y_1+D_1y_2, \qquad D_1\!\left(\frac{y_1}{y_2}\right)=D_1y_1-D_1y_2. \]

9.2 Constants

For any constant \(C>0\): \[ D_1(C)=0,\qquad D_1(Cy)=D_1y. \]

9.3 Power Rule

For any positive function \(y(x)\) and real \(a\): \[ D_1(y^a)=a\,D_1y. \]

9.4 Product and Quotient Rules

If \(y=u\,v\), then \(D_1y=D_1u+D_1v\). If \(y=u/v\), then \(D_1y=D_1u-D_1v\). \]

9.5 Relative Chain Rule

If \(y=f(u)\) and \(u=u(x)\), then: \[ D_1y=\frac{d(\ln y)}{d(\ln u)}\,D_1u. \]

9.6 From \(D_1\)-Equations to Classical ODEs

\[ D_1y=F(x,y) \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{y}{x}F(x,y). \]

9.7 Summary

  • \(D_1\) is linear in logarithmic form.
  • Products become sums; quotients become differences.
  • Power laws are trivial under \(D_1\).
  • \(D_1\)-equations reduce to linear classical equations.
