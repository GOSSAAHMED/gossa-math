9.1 Reminder: Definition and the Bridge to \(D_0\)

The relative derivative \(D_1\) measures the relative change of \(y\) against the relative change of \(x\). By definition:

\[ D_1 y \;=\; \frac{dy/y}{dx/x} \;=\; \frac{x}{y}\,\frac{dy}{dx} \;=\; \frac{x}{y}\,D_0 y. \]

Most properties in this chapter follow directly from this relation.

9.2 Invariance under Multiplication by a Constant

Let \(c\neq 0\) and \(\tilde{y}=c\,y\). Then:

\[ D_1(\tilde{y})=\frac{x}{\tilde{y}}\frac{d\tilde{y}}{dx} =\frac{x}{cy}\cdot c\frac{dy}{dx} =\frac{x}{y}\frac{dy}{dx}=D_1(y). \]
Since \(D_1\) measures ratios, scaling \(y\) by a constant does not change \(D_1\).

9.3 Relative Product Rule: \(D_1(uv)=D_1(u)+D_1(v)\)

Let \(y=uv\) with \(u\neq 0\) and \(v\neq 0\). Then:

\[ D_1(uv)=\frac{x}{uv}\frac{d(uv)}{dx} =\frac{x}{uv}\left(u\frac{dv}{dx}+v\frac{du}{dx}\right) =\frac{x}{v}\frac{dv}{dx}+\frac{x}{u}\frac{du}{dx}. \] \[ D_1(uv)=D_1(v)+D_1(u)=D_1(u)+D_1(v). \]
This shows a central structural fact: at the relative level, multiplication becomes additive.

9.4 Relative Quotient Rule

\[ D_1\!\left(\frac{u}{v}\right)=D_1(u)-D_1(v)\quad (v\neq 0). \]

This follows from the product rule by writing \(u/v=u\cdot v^{-1}\) and using the power rule below.

9.5 Relative Power Rule: \(D_1(u^a)=a\,D_1(u)\)

Let \(y=u^a\) with \(u\neq 0\) and constant \(a\). Then:

\[ D_1(u^a)=\frac{x}{u^a}\frac{d(u^a)}{dx} =\frac{x}{u^a}\left(a\,u^{a-1}\frac{du}{dx}\right) =a\,\frac{x}{u}\frac{du}{dx} =a\,D_1(u). \]
In particular, if \(y=x^a\), then \(D_1 y=a\): the exponent is revealed directly.

9.6 Chain Rule in Relative Form

If \(y=f(u)\) and \(u=g(x)\), then:

\[ D_1 y=\frac{x}{y}\frac{dy}{dx} =\frac{x}{y}\frac{dy}{du}\frac{du}{dx} =\left(\frac{u}{y}\frac{dy}{du}\right)\left(\frac{x}{u}\frac{du}{dx}\right). \]
\[ D_1^{(x)}(y)=D_1^{(u)}(y)\cdot D_1^{(x)}(u). \]

9.7 Useful Conversion Formulas: \(D_0 \leftrightarrow D_1\)

From \[ D_1 y=\frac{x}{y}D_0 y \] we obtain the inverse relation:

\[ D_0 y=\frac{y}{x}\,D_1 y. \]

These formulas are essential when rewriting equations across levels.

9.8 Typical Interpretations: Elasticity and Growth

In applications, \(D_1 y\) often plays the role of elasticity: how many percent \(y\) changes when \(x\) changes by one percent.

Power law: If \(y=Cx^a\), then \[ D_1 y=a, \] a constant elasticity independent of \(x\) and \(C\).
Exponential growth: If \(y=e^{kx}\), then \[ D_1 y=kx, \] meaning the relative response increases linearly with \(x\).

9.9 Additional Worked Examples

Example 1: Compute \(D_1\!\left(\dfrac{x^3}{1+x}\right)\) with \(x\neq 0\), \(1+x\neq 0\).
Solution: \[ D_1\!\left(\frac{x^3}{1+x}\right)=D_1(x^3)-D_1(1+x)=3-\frac{x}{1+x}. \]
Example 2: If \(y=\sqrt{x}\) (for \(x>0\)), compute \(D_1 y\).
Solution: Since \(y=x^{1/2}\), \[ D_1 y=\frac{1}{2}. \]
Example 3: If \(y=(x^2+1)^5\) with \(x\neq 0\), compute \(D_1 y\).
Solution: \[ D_1 y = \frac{x}{y}\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{x}{(x^2+1)^5}\cdot 5(x^2+1)^4\cdot 2x =\frac{10x^2}{x^2+1}. \]
