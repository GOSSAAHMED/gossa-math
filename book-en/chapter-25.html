Chapter 25 — Hierarchical Equations at Level \(D_2\)

Equations governing changes of relative change

25.1 Conceptual Overview

Level \(D_2\) analyzes how relative growth itself evolves. This level naturally handles highly nonlinear and super-exponential systems.

25.2 General Definition

A level-\(D_2\) hierarchical equation has the form: \[ F(x,y,D_1y,D_2y)=0. \]

25.3 Constant Deep Equation

\[ D_2y=a \Rightarrow y=\exp\!\big((\ln x)^a\big) \]

25.4 Nonlinear ODE Becomes Constant

\[ y'=y\ln y \Rightarrow D_2y=1 \]

25.5 Extreme Nonlinearity

\[ y'=y\ln y\ln\ln y \Rightarrow D_3y=1 \]

25.6 Numerical Examples

\(y'=y^2 \Rightarrow D_2y=1\)
\(y'=y(\ln y)^2 \Rightarrow D_2y=2\)
\(y'=y e^x \Rightarrow D_2y=x\)
\(y'=x y\ln y \Rightarrow D_2y=x\)
\(y'=y(\ln y)^n \Rightarrow D_2y=n\)

25.7 Structural Interpretation

  • Each higher level removes nonlinearity.
  • Constants reveal deep invariants.
  • Level \(D_2\) is a stability level.

25.8 Summary

  • Level \(D_2\) simplifies extreme nonlinear equations.
  • Super-exponential growth becomes tractable.
  • This prepares the general theory of \(D_n\).
