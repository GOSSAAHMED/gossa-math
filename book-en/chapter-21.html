21.1 The Main Trick: lift to the correct scale

Choose \(X=L_n(x)\) and \(Y=L_n(y)\) so that \(D_ny = dY/dX\). Then solve standard ODE types in \(Y(X)\) and invert back to \(y(x)\).

21.2 Dictionary

Level 1: \(X=\ln x\), \(Y=\ln y\) â‡’ \(D_1y=dY/dX\). Level 2: \(X=\ln\ln x\), \(Y=\ln\ln y\) â‡’ \(D_2y=dY/dX\). Level 3: \(X=\ln\ln\ln x\), \(Y=\ln\ln\ln y\) â‡’ \(D_3y=dY/dX\).

21.3 Type I: constant-level equations \(D_ny=a\)

  • \(D_1y=a \Rightarrow y=Cx^a\)
  • \(D_2y=a \Rightarrow y=\exp(A(\ln x)^a)\)
  • \(D_3y=a \Rightarrow y=\exp(\exp(A(\ln\ln x)^a))\)

21.4 Type II: \(D_ny=g(X)\) (separable on the scale)

If \(dY/dX=g(X)\), then \(Y=\int g(X)\,dX + C\), then invert \(L_n\).

21.5 Type III: linear ODEs at level \(n\)

\[ \frac{dY}{dX}+p(X)Y=q(X), \quad \mu(X)=e^{\int p(X)dX}. \] \[ Y\mu=\int q\mu\,dX+C. \]

21.6 Type IV: Bernoulli at level \(n\)

\(\frac{dY}{dX}+pY=qY^m\). Use \(Z=Y^{1-m}\) to reduce to linear.
