10.1 Why Go Beyond \(D_1\)?

The relative derivative \(D_1\) measures proportional change. However, in many systems the proportional structure itself evolves with scale. This motivates a deeper level of measurement.

10.2 Formal Definition of Level \(D_2\)

\[ \boxed{ D_2 y = \frac{d(\ln\ln y)}{d(\ln\ln x)} } \qquad (\ln x>0,\;\ln y>0) \]

This is a direct specialization of the general hierarchical definition \(D_n y=d(L_n(y))/d(L_n(x))\).

10.3 Computational Formula

\[ D_2 y = \frac{x\ln x}{y\ln y}\,\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{\ln x}{\ln y}\,D_1 y \]

10.4 Interpretation

  • \(D_1\): relative change.
  • \(D_2\): evolution of relative structure across scales.

10.5 Examples

Power law: \(y=x^a\Rightarrow D_2 y=1\).
Exponential growth: \(y=e^{kx}\Rightarrow D_2 y=\dfrac{\ln x}{\ln(kx)}kx\).
Composite growth: \(y=e^{x^2}\Rightarrow D_2 y=2\).

10.6 What \(D_2\) Reveals

  • Stability of relative laws.
  • Hidden scale transitions.
  • Distinction between apparent and intrinsic growth.
