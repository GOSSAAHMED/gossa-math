Chapter 24 — Hierarchical Equations at Level \(D_1\)

Differential equations expressed in relative form

24.1 Conceptual Overview

Level \(D_1\) equations describe relative change. Many classical nonlinear equations become linear or constant at this level.

24.2 General Definition

A level-\(D_1\) hierarchical equation has the form: \[ F(x,y,D_1y)=0, \qquad D_1y=\frac{d(\ln y)}{d(\ln x)}. \]

24.3 Constant Relative Equation

\[ D_1y=a \Rightarrow y=Cx^a \]

24.4 Variable Relative Equation

\[ D_1y=f(x) \Rightarrow \ln y=\int f(x)\,d(\ln x)+C \]

24.5 Numerical Examples

\(D_1y=2 \Rightarrow y=Cx^2\)
\(D_1y=-1 \Rightarrow y=C/x\)
\(D_1y=x \Rightarrow y=Ce^x\)
\(D_1y=\ln x \Rightarrow y=e^{(\ln x)^2/2}\)
\(D_1y=a+b/\ln x \Rightarrow y=Cx^a(\ln x)^b\)

24.6 Structural Interpretation

  • Equations live in log–log space.
  • Solutions are straight or gently curved.
  • Constants correspond to geometric slopes.

24.7 Summary

  • Level \(D_1\) equations are simple and powerful.
  • Many classical ODEs reduce to constants.
  • This is the natural first hierarchical level.
