17.1 Why Discrete Data?

In real experiments and field measurements we observe samples \((x_i,y_i)\). The goal is to detect which hierarchical level provides the simplest growth law: relative (\(D_1\)) vs nested-log (\(D_2\), \(D_3\)).

17.2 Discrete Hierarchical Derivatives

Two-point estimate of \(D_1\): \[ \widehat{D_1}= \frac{\ln y_{i+1}-\ln y_i}{\ln x_{i+1}-\ln x_i}, \quad (x_i>0,\ y_i>0). \]
Two-point estimate of \(D_2\): \[ \widehat{D_2}= \frac{\ln\ln y_{i+1}-\ln\ln y_i}{\ln\ln x_{i+1}-\ln\ln x_i}, \quad (\ln x_i>0,\ \ln y_i>0). \]
Two-point estimate of \(D_3\): \[ \widehat{D_3}= \frac{\ln\ln\ln y_{i+1}-\ln\ln\ln y_i}{\ln\ln\ln x_{i+1}-\ln\ln\ln x_i}, \quad (\ln\ln x_i>0,\ \ln\ln y_i>0). \]

17.3 Automatic Level Selection (Decision Algorithm)

  1. Compute \(\widehat{D_1}\) on all segments.
  2. Measure spread (range or mean absolute deviation).
  3. If \(\widehat{D_1}\) is stable ‚Üí choose \(D_1\).
  4. Else, if domain allows, compute \(\widehat{D_2}\) and compare stability.
  5. If \(\widehat{D_2}\) is much more stable ‚Üí choose \(D_2\).
  6. Else, if domain allows, compute \(\widehat{D_3}\) ‚Üí choose the first stable level.

17.4 Two Simple Stability Metrics

\[ R_n=\max \widehat{D_n}-\min \widehat{D_n},\qquad M_n=\frac{1}{m}\sum|\widehat{D_n}-\overline{D_n}|. \]

Examples 11‚Äì18 ‚Äî Quick guidance cases

  • Deep-scale jumps suggest \(D_3\) stability checks.
  • If \(\ln\ln\) is not defined (values near 1), drop to \(D_1\) or rescale.
  • If no level stabilizes, the phenomenon may not be nested-log-type.

17.6 Summary

  • Discrete \(\widehat{D_n}\) is a direct finite-difference translation of the formal definition.
  • Select the level that minimizes spread (range/deviation) of \(\widehat{D_n}\).
  • Always respect domain constraints for \(\ln\ln\) and \(\ln\ln\ln\).
