Chapter 17 — Logarithmic Integration

Reconstructing deep growth laws (inverse of \(D_2\))

17.1 Conceptual Overview

Logarithmic integration reconstructs a function from the evolution of its relative growth itself.

17.2 Formal Definition

If \[ D_2y=g(x), \] then \[ \ln\ln y=\int g(x)\, d(\ln\ln x)+C. \]

17.3 Why Logarithmic Integration Matters

  • Handles super-exponential growth.
  • Reveals hidden stability.
  • Simplifies highly nonlinear equations.

17.4 Fifteen Numerical Examples

1. \(D_2y=1 \Rightarrow y=x^C\)
2. \(D_2y=0 \Rightarrow y=e^{e^C}\)
3. \(D_2y=\ln x\)
4. \(D_2y=x\)
5. \(D_2y=1/\ln x\)
6. \(D_2y=a \Rightarrow y=\exp((\ln x)^a)\)
7. \(D_2y=\ln\ln x\)
8. \(D_2y=\sin(\ln\ln x)\)
9. \(D_2y=(\ln x)^n\)
10. \(D_2y=1/x\)
11. \(D_2y=e^{\ln\ln x}\)
12. \(D_2y=\ln(x^2+1)\)
13. \(D_2y=f(\ln\ln x)\)
14. \(D_2y=\ln\ln x/\ln x\)
15. \(D_2y=1+1/\ln\ln x\)

17.5 Interpretation

  • Integration in \((\ln\ln x,\ln\ln y)\) space.
  • Rebuilds deep curvature.
  • Natural for complex systems.

17.6 Summary

  • Logarithmic integration is the inverse of \(D_2\).
  • It handles deep growth structures.
  • Essential for hierarchical differential equations.
