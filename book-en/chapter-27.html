Chapter 27 — Stability, Growth, and Blow-Up

Hierarchical classification of dynamical behavior

27.1 Conceptual Introduction

In hierarchical calculus, stability depends on the level of description. Explosive behavior at one level may become stable at a higher level.

27.2 Hierarchical Definitions

  • Stable: derivatives are constant or zero at some level.
  • Growth: bounded, non-constant behavior.
  • Blow-up: divergence at all levels.

27.4 True Blow-Up

True blow-up occurs when \[ D_ny \to \infty \quad \forall n. \]

27.5 Interpretation

  • Stability is scale-dependent.
  • Growth is structural, not pathological.
  • Blow-up signals model failure.

27.6 Summary

  • Hierarchical calculus refines stability analysis.
  • Many apparent blow-ups disappear at higher levels.
  • This enables robust modeling of complex systems.
