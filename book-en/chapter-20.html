20.1 Why Taylor (D0) may fail outside a small neighborhood

Taylor expands on the additive scale of \(x\). If the phenomenon is essentially multiplicative or nested-log, the natural scale is \(\ln x\) or \(\ln\ln x\). So we expand \(L_n(y)\) as a Taylor series in \(L_n(x)\).

20.2 Level-1 approximation: linear \(\ln y\) vs \(\ln x\)

If \(\ln y \approx a\ln x + b\), then \(y\approx e^b x^a\). This matches power-law behavior over wide ranges.

20.3 Level-2 approximation: linear \(\ln\ln y\) vs \(\ln\ln x\)

If \(\ln\ln y \approx a\ln\ln x + c\), then \(y\approx \exp(A(\ln x)^a)\).

20.4 Level-3 approximation: linear \(\ln\ln\ln y\) vs \(\ln\ln\ln x\)

If \(\ln\ln\ln y \approx a\ln\ln\ln x + c\), then \(y\approx \exp(\exp(A(\ln\ln x)^a))\).

20.5 A General ‚ÄúHierarchical Taylor‚Äù

Let \(X=L_n(x)\), \(Y=L_n(y)\). Expand: \[ Y(X)\approx Y(X_0)+Y'(X_0)(X-X_0)+\frac{Y''(X_0)}{2}(X-X_0)^2+\cdots \] Then invert \(L_n\) to recover \(y\).

20.6 Twenty-Four Examples (Conceptual Comparisons)

  • Power laws: exact linearity at level \(D_1\).
  • \(\exp((\ln x)^a)\)-type growth: stability at \(D_2\).
  • \(\exp(\exp((\ln\ln x)^a))\)-type growth: stability at \(D_3\).
  • Classical smooth functions near 0: Taylor (D0) remains best.

20.7 Practical Recipe

  1. Compute \(\widehat{D_1},\widehat{D_2},\widehat{D_3}\) from data.
  2. Select the most stable level.
  3. Fit a low-order polynomial for \(Y\) vs \(X\) at that level.
  4. Invert back to obtain \(y(x)\), validate on new points.
