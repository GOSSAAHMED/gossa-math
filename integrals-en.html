1) Definition: the hierarchical integral \(\mathcal{I}_n\)

\(\mathcal{I}_n\) is the natural inverse of the hierarchical derivative \(D_n\) (on a suitable domain, up to a hierarchical integration constant).

\[ \boxed{D_n\big(\mathcal{I}_n[f]\big)=f} \]

2) Fundamental theorem of hierarchical integration

2.1) Left-inverse property

\[\boxed{D_n(\mathcal{I}_n[f])=f}\]

2.2) Right-inverse property (up to a hierarchical constant)

If \(D_n F=f\), then:

\[ \boxed{\mathcal{I}_n[D_n F]=F\;\odot\;C_n} \]

The constant and the operation depend on the level:

LevelConstant formOperation \(\odot\)
0Additive constant \(C\)\(+\)
1Multiplicative constant \(C_\times>0\)\(\times\)
2Structural/exponential constant \(C_{\exp}>1\)exponential-level composition
Intuition: each level has its own notion of accumulation (additive → multiplicative → structural).

3) Relative integral \(\mathcal{I}_1\) — 5 examples

Practical formula:

\[ \boxed{\mathcal{I}_1[f](x)=\exp\!\left(\int \frac{f(x)}{x}\,dx\right)} \]
\(f(x)\)\(\mathcal{I}_1[f](x)\)Quick check
\(1\)\(x\)\(D_1(x)=1\)
\(a\) (constant)\(x^a\)\(D_1(x^a)=a\)
\(\ln x\)\(e^{(\ln x)^2/2}\)\(D_1(\cdot)=\ln x\)
\(x^k\)\(e^{x^k/k}\ (k\neq0)\)\(D_1(\cdot)=x^k\)
\(\frac{1}{\ln x}\)\(\ln x\)\(D_1(\ln x)=\frac1{\ln x}\)

4) Logarithmic integral \(\mathcal{I}_2\) — 5 examples

For \(x>1\) (so \(\ln x>0\)), a practical formula:

\[ \boxed{ \mathcal{I}_2[f](x)= \exp\!\left( \exp\!\left(\int \frac{f(x)}{x\,\ln x}\,dx\right) \right) } \]
\(f(x)\)\(\mathcal{I}_2[f](x)\)Quick check
\(1\)\(x\)\(D_2(x)=1\)
\(a\) (constant)\(e^{(\ln x)^a}\)\(D_2(\cdot)=a\)
\(\ln\ln x\)\(\exp(\exp((\ln\ln x)^2/2))\)\(D_2(\cdot)=\ln\ln x\)
\(\ln x\)\(e^{x}\)\(D_2(e^x)=\ln x\)
\((\ln\ln x)^n\)\(\exp(\exp((\ln\ln x)^{n+1}/(n+1)))\)\(D_2(\cdot)=(\ln\ln x)^n\)
At level 2, accumulation naturally involves iterated exponential structure because the measuring scale is \(\ln\ln x\).
