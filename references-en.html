How this connects
Hierarchical lifting can be interpreted as choosing a coordinate system where the problem is better conditioned, then transforming back (often via iterated exponentiation).

5) Domain Assumptions (important for rigor)

- Level 1: typically requires x>0 and f(x)>0 when using ln x and ln f.
- Level 2: requires x>1 with ln(x)>0 and f(x)>1 with ln(f)>0.
- Level 3+: adds further conditions for iterated logarithms and avoids singularities.
