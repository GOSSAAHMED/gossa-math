Introduction

In Hierarchical Calculus, the derivatives \(D_0, D_1, D_2, \dots\) are not independent operators. They represent interconnected levels within a single derivative hierarchy.

This page presents the explicit mathematical formulas that relate these hierarchical derivatives directly from their definitions.

General Definition

For a function \(y=y(x)\), the hierarchical derivative of order \(n\) is:

\[ D_n y = \frac{dy}{dx}\; \frac{x\,\phi_n(x)}{y\,\phi_n(y)}. \]

Hierarchical scaling functions:

\[ \phi_1(t)=1,\quad \phi_2(t)=\ln t,\quad \phi_3(t)=\ln\ln t,\;\dots \]

First Levels

\[ D_0 y=\frac{dy}{dx},\qquad D_1 y=\frac{dy\cdot x}{dx\cdot y},\qquad D_2 y=\frac{dy\cdot x\ln x}{dx\cdot y\ln y}. \]

Relation with the Classical Derivative

\[ \boxed{ D_n y = D_0 y \;\frac{x\,\phi_n(x)}{y\,\phi_n(y)} } \]

Relation Between Consecutive Levels

\[ \boxed{ D_{n+1} y = D_n y \;\frac{\phi_{n+1}(x)}{\phi_n(x)} \;\frac{\phi_n(y)}{\phi_{n+1}(y)} } \]

Low-Level Examples

\[ D_1 y = D_0 y\;\frac{x}{y} \]
\[ D_2 y = D_1 y\;\frac{\ln x}{\ln y} \]
\[ D_3 y = D_2 y\;\frac{\ln\ln x}{\ln\ln y} \]

Compact Hierarchical Form

\[ \boxed{ D_n y = D_1 y \prod_{k=2}^{n} \frac{\phi_k(x)}{\phi_k(y)} } \]
\[ \boxed{ D_n y = D_0 y \;\frac{x}{y} \prod_{k=2}^{n} \frac{\phi_k(x)}{\phi_k(y)} } \]
