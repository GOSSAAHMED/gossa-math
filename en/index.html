Hierarchical Calculus
Multi-Level Derivatives and Integrals

Hierarchical Calculus is a completely new mathematical framework that introduces a family of derivatives, integrals, series, and differential equations across multiple scales: differential (D₀), relative (D₁), logarithmic (D₂), and hyper-logarithmic (D₃, Dₙ), with applications in cosmology, medicine, economics, language, and probability.

What is Hierarchical Calculus?

Hierarchical Calculus generalizes classical differential calculus to a full hierarchy of derivatives D₀, D₁, D₂, D₃, …, Dₙ, where each level captures a different type of change in time and scale.

Core Derivatives

  • D₀: classical derivative D₀ y = dy/dx
  • D₁: relative derivative D₁ y = (x / y) · D₀ y
  • D₂: logarithmic derivative D₂ y = (x ln x / (y ln y)) · D₀ y
  • Dₙ: general formula Dₙ y = [Lₙ(x) / Lₙ(y)] · D₀ y

Here Lₙ(x) = x · ln(x) · lnln(x) · … is the n-th hierarchical logarithmic ladder.

The Golden Relation

The entire hierarchy is built from a single fundamental identity discovered by Ahmed Gossa:

x · D₀(y) = y · D₁(y)

This “golden relation” links the classical derivative to the relative one and allows constructing all higher-order hierarchical derivatives Dₙ and their connections.

Applications of Hierarchical Calculus

The theory extends far beyond pure mathematics and offers a unified language for multiscale phenomena in many fields.

Cosmology & Astrophysics

Reformulating cosmic expansion, dark energy, and dark matter using D₂ and D₃ instead of a single Hubble parameter or cosmological constant.

Medicine & Biology

Modelling cancer growth, immune response, ageing, and tissue regeneration across fast (D₀), relative (D₁), and slow (D₂, D₃) biological timescales.

Economics & Finance

Describing economic growth, inflation, crises, and long-term cycles using hierarchical derivatives for short, medium, and long horizons.

Language & Qur’anic Rhetoric

A hierarchical linguistic model (letter–word–sentence–text) and a new mathematical approach to analysing the inimitable rhetoric of the Qur’an, especially Sūrat al-Fātiḥa.

Hierarchical Probability

New probability measures, expectations, and risks based on hierarchical measures dμₙ = dx / Lₙ(x), covering both short-term and deep-time uncertainty.

Future Prediction

A multilevel forecasting framework that chooses the appropriate Dₙ level for each phenomenon – from markets to civilizations and the universe.

About the Author – Ahmed Gossa

Founder of the Relative Calculus and Hierarchical Calculus frameworks, and discoverer of the golden relation between classical and relative derivatives.

Short Biography

Ahmed Gossa is an independent Arab researcher in mathematics and theoretical thinking. Over many years, he developed the concept of the relative derivative, then generalized it to logarithmic and hierarchical derivatives, arriving at the general formula:

Dₙ y = [Lₙ(x) / Lₙ(y)] · D₀ y

He applied this structure to multiple levels of reality: from atoms to galaxies, from cells to societies, from words to Qur’anic texts, and from markets to the evolution of the universe.
