What is Hierarchical Calculus?

Hierarchical Calculus is a framework that organizes differentiation and integration across levels of scale. It contains:

  • \(D_0\): classical differential derivative.
  • \(D_1\): relative derivative (scale derivative).
  • \(D_2\): logarithmic-scale derivative.
  • Higher levels \(D_n\) built by hierarchical scaling.
Core idea: many expressions become simpler when written on the correct scale (additive vs relative vs logarithmic).
