Series: Differential, Relative, and Logarithmic

This page unifies three approximation families around a reference point \(x_0\): (1) the classical differential Taylor series (rank 0, operator \(D_0^n\)), (2) a relative multiplicative (“product”) Taylor series (rank 1, operator \(D_1^n\)), (3) a rank-2 logarithmic exponential series (rank 2, operator \(D_2^n\)). We always write derivatives with both rank and degree.

rank 0: differential rank 1: relative (product) rank 2: log-rank (exponential) Function approximation

Core Concepts

Expansion point

Choose \(x_0\) inside the domain. Approximation quality depends on the notion of “closeness”:
— For rank 0: additive closeness via \(x-x_0\).
— For rank 1: relative closeness via \(x/x_0\).
— For rank 2: logarithmic closeness via \(\ln x/\ln x_0\) (equivalently \(\ln(\ln x)\)).

Domain note: Rank \(D_2\) uses \(\ln(\ln x)\) and \(\ln(\ln f)\), hence we assume \(x>1\) (so \(\ln x>0\)) and \(f(x)>1\) (so \(\ln f>0\)).
