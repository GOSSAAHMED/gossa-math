Solved Examples of Hierarchical Equations

This page presents worked examples of hierarchical equations.
Notation rule: every derivative is written with its rank and degree: \(\;D_r^n\).

Example 1: Classical differential equation

\[ D_{0}^{1}y(x)=3\,y(x) \]
\[ y(x)=C\,e^{3x} \]

Example 2: Relative (rank-1) equation

\[ D_{1}^{1}y(x)=k \]
\[ \frac{d\ln y}{d\ln x}=k \;\Longrightarrow\; y(x)=C\,x^{k}. \]

Example 3: Logarithmic (rank-2) equation

\[ D_{2}^{1}y(x)=A \]
\[ \frac{d\ln(\ln y)}{d\ln(\ln x)}=A \;\Longrightarrow\; y(x)=\exp\!\big(C\,(\ln x)^{A}\big), \quad x>1. \]

Example 4: Successive degrees within rank 1

\[ D_{1}^{1}y(x)=a \]
\[ D_{1}^{2}y(x)=D_{1}^{1}\!\big(D_{1}^{1}y\big)=0, \qquad D_{1}^{3}y(x)=0. \]

Methodology for Solving Mixed-Rank Equations

When an equation mixes several ranks, the key idea is to rewrite it in a single “derivative language”, solve it there, and then return to \(y(x)\).

\[ D_{1}^{1}y=\frac{x}{y}D_{0}^{1}y \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad D_{0}^{1}y=\frac{y}{x}D_{1}^{1}y. \]
  1. Identify the ranks present.
  2. Choose a working rank.
  3. Convert the others using bridge relations.
  4. Solve and check domains.
Remark: power-law models are often simplest at rank 1; multi-scale behavior may suggest rank 2.

Example 5A: Mixing \(D_{0}^{1}\) and \(D_{1}^{1}\)

\[ D_{1}^{1}y(x)+D_{0}^{1}y(x)=x \]

Converting to a single rank shows the equation is nonlinear without further assumptions. This example illustrates the importance of choosing an appropriate representation.

Example 6: Higher degree at rank 0

\[ D_{0}^{2}y(x)=k^{2}y(x) \]
\[ y(x)=C_1e^{kx}+C_2e^{-kx}. \]

Example 7A: Linking rank 1 and rank 2

\[ D_{1}^{1}y(x)-D_{2}^{1}y(x)=0 \]
\[ u=\ln x,\; z=\ln y \;\Rightarrow\; \frac{dz}{du}-\frac{d\ln z}{d\ln u}=0 \;\Rightarrow\; y(x)=\text{constant}\ \text{or}\ y(x)=x,\; x>1. \]
