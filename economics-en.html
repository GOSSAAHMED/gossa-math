Hierarchical Calculus in Economics

Economics is inherently relative: growth, inflation, returns, elasticity, and risk are naturally read as ratios. The classical derivative \(D_0^1\) measures absolute change, while \(D_1^1\) captures relative structural change (elasticity-like behavior). The rank-2 hierarchical layer \(D_2^1\) captures acceleration of relative change, which often appears in bubbles, crises, and runaway risk regimes.

Notation: We write derivatives explicitly as \(D_r^1\). \(D_0^1\) classical, \(D_1^1\) relative, \(D_2^1\) logarithmic (rank-2), up to \(D_n^1\).

How to interpret ranks in economics

Rank Operator (quick view) Economic meaning Fast numeric reading
0 \(D_0^1 y \approx \dfrac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}\) Absolute change Difference/slope
1 \(D_1^1 y \approx \dfrac{\Delta y/y}{\Delta x/x}\) Relative change (elasticity) %\(y\) per %\(x\)
2 \(D_2^1 y\) (rank-2 behavior) Acceleration of relative change Is the “% change” itself speeding up?

Practical note: the strict \(\ln\ln\) domain of \(D_2^1\) is naturally satisfied when working with positive indices (risk indices, price indices, utilization-like ratios). In economics, rank-2 is used as a structured descriptor for “acceleration of relative rates”.

20 applications with numeric examples

Reading tip: Compare \(D_0^1\) (raw numbers) vs \(D_1^1\) (relative/elastic). When relative rates accelerate, you are in the rank-2 layer \(D_2^1\).

1) GDP growth

From 200 to 220 (billion).

\[ D_0^1:\;\Delta GDP=20 \qquad D_1^1:\;\frac{20}{200}=10\% \]

2) Inflation (CPI)

Price index from 100 to 108.

\[ D_0^1:\;+8 \qquad D_1^1:\;\frac{8}{100}=8\% \]

3) Nominal vs real growth

Nominal GDP growth 10%, inflation 6%.

\[ \text{Approx. real growth} \approx 10\%-6\%=4\% \]

4) Exchange rate move

USD from 100 to 115 (local currency).

\[ D_0^1:\;+15 \qquad D_1^1:\;\frac{15}{100}=15\% \]

5) Trade deficit growth

Deficit from 5 to 7 (billion).

\[ D_0^1:\;+2 \qquad D_1^1:\;\frac{2}{5}=40\% \]

6) Debt-to-GDP ratio

Debt 120, GDP 200 → 0.60. Then debt 132, GDP 204.

\[ \text{Before: }0.60 \quad \text{After: }\frac{132}{204}\approx0.647 \quad\Rightarrow\quad \frac{0.647-0.60}{0.60}\approx 7.8\% \]

7) Compounded interest

100 at 5% yearly for 2 years.

\[ 100\to 105\to 110.25 \qquad \text{Rank-1 accumulation is the natural language here.} \]

8) Discounting (PV)

120 in 1 year, discount rate 10%.

\[ PV=\frac{120}{1.10}\approx 109.09 \]

9) Unemployment as relative change

Unemployment from 10% to 12%.

\[ D_0^1:\;+2 \text{ points} \qquad D_1^1:\;\frac{12-10}{10}=20\% \]

10) Wage–price spiral signal (rank-2)

Inflation 4% then 8% quickly.

\[ \frac{8}{4}=2 \quad\Rightarrow\quad \text{acceleration of relative rate (rank-2 behavior)} \]

11) Asset bubble signature

Monthly relative growth: 2% → 5% → 12%.

\[ \text{Relative rates accelerate}\;\Rightarrow\; \text{rank-2 warning layer} \]

12) Risk premium jump

Risk premium from 3% to 4.5%.

\[ D_0^1:\;+1.5\text{ points} \qquad D_1^1:\;\frac{4.5-3}{3}=50\% \]

13) Volatility index move

Index from 16 to 24.

\[ D_0^1:\;+8 \qquad D_1^1:\;\frac{8}{16}=50\% \]

14) Price elasticity of demand

Price +10%, quantity −15%.

\[ E \approx \frac{-15\%}{10\%}=-1.5 \quad (\text{rank-1 / elasticity}) \]

15) Supply elasticity

Price +8%, supply +4%.

\[ E_s \approx \frac{4\%}{8\%}=0.5 \]

16) Marginal propensity to consume

Income +1000, consumption +700.

\[ D_0^1:\;\Delta C/\Delta Y = 0.7 \]

17) Productivity improvement

Output 500→540, hours 100→105.

\[ \text{Prod. before}=5 \quad \text{after}\approx \frac{540}{105}=5.1429 \Rightarrow \frac{5.1429-5}{5}\approx 2.86\% \]

18) Positive inequality/risk index

Positive index \(R\): 1.20→1.35.

\[ D_0^1:\;+0.15 \qquad D_1^1:\;\frac{0.15}{1.20}=12.5\% \]

19) Platform/technology adoption

Users 1.0M→1.3M in a month.

\[ D_0^1:\;+0.3M \qquad D_1^1:\;\frac{0.3}{1.0}=30\% \]

20) Program/project cost overrun

Cost 10M→12M.

\[ D_0^1:\;+2M \qquad D_1^1:\;\frac{2}{10}=20\% \quad\Rightarrow\quad \text{scale-aware alarm} \]

Quick decision guide

Question Recommended rank Why
How much changed? \(D_0^1\) Raw reporting
How much changed relatively / elasticity? \\(D_1^1\) Scale-invariant economics
Are relative rates accelerating? \(D_2^1\) Bubbles, crises, runaway risk
Multi-phase complex regimes \(D_n^1\) Higher hierarchical analysis
Takeaway: \(D_0^1\) = change, \(D_1^1\) = relative change (elasticity), \(D_2^1\) = acceleration of relative change.
