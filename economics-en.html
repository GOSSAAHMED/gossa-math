Hierarchical Calculus in Economics

Economics is naturally relative: growth, inflation, returns, exchange rates, elasticity, and risk are best interpreted through ratios and percent changes. Hierarchical Calculus formalizes this by organizing change into ranks: absolute change (\(D_0^1\)), relative/elastic change (\(D_1^1\)), and acceleration of relative change (\(D_2^1\)). This rank-2 layer often appears during bubbles, stress, and runaway risk regimes.

Rank 0: absolute change Rank 1: elasticity / relative rates Rank 2: acceleration of relative rates Beyond: multi-phase regimes

1) How to interpret ranks in economics

Core intuition
Rank 0 answers “how much did it move?”
Rank 1 answers “how much did it move in percent?” (elasticity-like)
Rank 2 answers “is the percent rate itself accelerating?”
Rank Operator (quick view) Economic meaning Fast numeric reading
0 \(D_0^1 y \approx \dfrac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}\) Absolute change Difference / slope
1 \(D_1^1 y \approx \dfrac{\Delta y/y}{\Delta x/x}\) Relative change (elasticity) %\(y\) per %\(x\)
2 \(D_2^1 y\) (rank-2 layer) Acceleration of relative change Is the “% change” speeding up?

Practical note: in economics, the rank-2 layer is used as a structured descriptor for “acceleration of relative rates,” especially in markets and risk indicators.

Notation: We write derivatives explicitly as \(D_r^1\). \(D_0^1\) is classical, \(D_1^1\) is relative, \(D_2^1\) is rank-2 behavior, and beyond is \(D_n^1\).

2) 20 applications with numeric examples

Reading tip: Compare \(D_0^1\) (raw values) vs \(D_1^1\) (percent/elastic values). If relative rates accelerate, the system enters the rank-2 layer \(D_2^1\).

1) GDP growth

GDP from 200 to 220 (billion).

\[ D_0^1:\;\Delta GDP=20 \qquad D_1^1:\;\frac{20}{200}=10\% \]

2) Inflation (CPI)

Price index from 100 to 108.

\[ D_0^1:\;+8 \qquad D_1^1:\;\frac{8}{100}=8\% \]

3) Nominal vs real growth

Nominal growth 10%, inflation 6%.

\[ \text{Approx. real growth} \approx 10\%-6\%=4\% \]

4) Exchange rate move

USD from 100 to 115 (local currency).

\[ D_0^1:\;+15 \qquad D_1^1:\;\frac{15}{100}=15\% \]

5) Trade deficit growth

Deficit from 5 to 7 (billion).

\[ D_0^1:\;+2 \qquad D_1^1:\;\frac{2}{5}=40\% \]

6) Debt-to-GDP ratio

Debt 120, GDP 200 → 0.60. Then debt 132, GDP 204.

\[ \text{Before: }0.60 \quad \text{After: }\frac{132}{204}\approx0.647 \quad\Rightarrow\quad \frac{0.647-0.60}{0.60}\approx 7.8\% \]

7) Compounded interest

100 at 5% yearly for 2 years.

\[ 100\to 105\to 110.25 \qquad (\text{Rank 1 is natural for compounding.}) \]

8) Discounting (present value)

120 in 1 year, discount rate 10%.

\[ PV=\frac{120}{1.10}\approx 109.09 \]

9) Unemployment as relative change

Unemployment from 10% to 12%.

\[ D_0^1:\;+2 \text{ points} \qquad D_1^1:\;\frac{12-10}{10}=20\% \]

10) Wage–price spiral signal (rank-2)

Inflation 4% then 8% quickly.

\[ \frac{8}{4}=2 \quad\Rightarrow\quad \text{relative rate doubled (rank-2 warning)} \]

11) Asset bubble signature

Monthly growth: 2% → 5% → 12%.

\[ \text{Relative rates accelerate}\;\Rightarrow\; \text{rank-2 instability layer} \]

12) Risk premium jump

Risk premium from 3% to 4.5%.

\[ D_0^1:\;+1.5\text{ points} \qquad D_1^1:\;\frac{4.5-3}{3}=50\% \]

13) Volatility index move

Index from 16 to 24.

\[ D_0^1:\;+8 \qquad D_1^1:\;\frac{8}{16}=50\% \]

14) Price elasticity of demand

Price +10%, quantity −15%.

\[ E \approx \frac{-15\%}{10\%}=-1.5 \]

15) Supply elasticity

Price +8%, supply +4%.

\[ E_s \approx \frac{4\%}{8\%}=0.5 \]

16) Marginal propensity to consume

Income +1000, consumption +700.

\[ MPC=\frac{\Delta C}{\Delta Y}=\frac{700}{1000}=0.7 \]

17) Productivity improvement

Output 500→540, hours 100→105.

\[ \text{Before}=5,\quad \text{After}\approx \frac{540}{105}=5.1429 \Rightarrow \frac{5.1429-5}{5}\approx 2.86\% \]

18) Positive inequality/risk index

Index \(R\): 1.20→1.35.

\[ D_0^1:\;+0.15 \qquad D_1^1:\;\frac{0.15}{1.20}=12.5\% \]

19) Technology adoption (platform growth)

Users 1.0M→1.3M in a month.

\[ D_0^1:\;+0.3M \qquad D_1^1:\;\frac{0.3}{1.0}=30\% \]

20) Project cost overrun

Cost 10M→12M.

\[ D_0^1:\;+2M \qquad D_1^1:\;\frac{2}{10}=20\% \quad\Rightarrow\quad \text{scale-aware alarm} \]

3) Quick decision guide

Question Recommended rank Why
How much changed? \(D_0^1\) Raw reporting (levels and differences)
How much changed in percent / elasticity? \(D_1^1\) Scale-invariant economics, rates, and growth
Are relative rates accelerating? \(D_2^1\) Bubbles, crises, runaway risk dynamics
Multi-phase complex regimes \(D_n^1\) Higher hierarchical analysis (multiple transitions)
Takeaway: \(D_0^1\) = change, \(D_1^1\) = relative change (elasticity), \(D_2^1\) = acceleration of relative change.
