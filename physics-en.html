üß™ Thermodynamics Application: Adiabatic Process (Ideal Gas)

This example shows why the Level 1 (relative) language is often the natural one in physics: many laws relate ratios, not differences.

1) Physical law

For an ideal gas undergoing an adiabatic process:

\[ P\,V^{\gamma}=\text{constant}, \qquad \gamma=\frac{C_p}{C_v} \]

where \(P\) is pressure, \(V\) is volume, and \(\gamma\) is the heat-capacity ratio.

2) Classical differential form (Level 0 / D0)

Taking logarithms and differentiating:

\[ \ln P + \gamma \ln V = \text{constant} \] \[ \frac{dP}{P}+\gamma\frac{dV}{V}=0 \quad\Rightarrow\quad \frac{dP}{dV}=-\gamma\frac{P}{V} \]

This is an ODE in \(P(V)\). It is simple, but it is not ‚Äúlinear‚Äù in the original variables.

3) Hierarchical (relative) form (Level 1 / D1)

Define the relative derivative:

\[ D_1 P(V)=\frac{d\ln P}{d\ln V} \]

From \(\ln P = -\gamma \ln V + \text{constant}\), we immediately get:

\[ \boxed{D_1 P = -\gamma} \]
Key observation
The hierarchical (Level 1) equation collapses to a constant. This reflects that the law is naturally expressed in ratios (log coordinates).

4) Reconstructing the solution

If \(D_1 P=-\gamma\) is constant, then:

\[ P(V)=P_0\left(\frac{V}{V_0}\right)^{-\gamma} \]

which is the standard physical solution.

5) Comparison (review-friendly)

Approach Equation Nature
Classical (D0) \(\dfrac{dP}{dV}=-\gamma\dfrac{P}{V}\) Differential equation in \(P(V)\)
Hierarchical (D1) \(D_1 P=-\gamma\) Constant (linear in log coordinates)

6) Interpretation

Hierarchical Calculus does not change physics; it selects the coordinate system (language of change) that matches the structure of the law. For adiabatic processes, the natural structure is relative: pressure changes proportionally to volume on a logarithmic scale.
