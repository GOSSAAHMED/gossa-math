What is Hierarchical Calculus?

Hierarchical Calculus is a mathematical framework that extends the concept of differentiation by introducing a structured hierarchy of derivatives. Instead of treating all derivatives as a single uniform family, it organizes derivatives using the notions of rank and degree.

Why was it introduced?

  • Some differential equations appear unsolvable under classical methods.
  • Complex systems can involve layered change that classical derivatives cannot separate clearly.
  • A richer operator structure can help transform and classify equations.

Key idea: Rank and Degree

In hierarchical calculus, a derivative is not only “first” or “second”. It can be characterized by:

  • Rank: the structural layer of differentiation
  • Degree: the intensity/power of the operator within that rank
