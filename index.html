    Core Definitions

    We denote \(D_0^{(1)}\) as the rank-0 (differential) derivative and \(D_1^{(1)}\) as the rank-1 (relative) derivative. In general, we write hierarchical derivatives with explicit rank and degree as \(D_n^{(k)}\), with \(k=1,2,3,\dots\). The hierarchy is expressed with iterated logarithms.

    Definition General

    The level-\(n\) hierarchical derivative (domain conditions apply):

    \[ D_n^{(1)} f(x)=\frac{d\,\ln^{(n)}(f(x))}{d\,\ln^{(n)}(x)} \]

    where \(\ln^{(n)}\) denotes the iterated logarithm (e.g., \(\ln^{(3)}(x)=\ln(\ln(\ln x))\)).

    Hierarchical Equations

    Hierarchical equations generalize differential equations by using derivatives of level \(n\). General form:

    \[ D_n y = F(x,y) \]
    Example Sketch

    A guiding example of a blow-up type equation:

    \[ \frac{dy}{dx}=y\ln y \]

    With an appropriate hierarchical transformation, one may obtain a simpler expression in a higher level (details in the equations page).
