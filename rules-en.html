1) Quick definition of the relative derivative \(D_1\)

Assume \(x>0\) and \(f(x)>0\). The relative derivative measures change with respect to a relative scale.

\[ \boxed{D_1 f(x)=\frac{d}{d\ln x}\big(\ln f(x)\big)=\frac{d\ln f(x)}{d\ln x}} \]
If \(D_1 f\) is constant, then \(f\) follows a power-law structure \(f(x)\propto x^\alpha\).

2) Core rules for \(D_1\)

2.1) Multiplying by a constant

\[ \boxed{D_1(Cf)=D_1 f \qquad (C>0\ \text{constant})} \]

Because \(\ln(Cf)=\ln C+\ln f\) and \(d(\ln C)/d(\ln x)=0\).

2.2) Product rule (relative superposition)

\[ \boxed{D_1(fg)=D_1 f + D_1 g} \]

2.3) Quotient rule

\[ \boxed{D_1\!\left(\frac{f}{g}\right)=D_1 f - D_1 g} \]

2.4) Constant power

\[ \boxed{D_1(f^a)=a\,D_1 f \qquad (a\ \text{constant})} \]

2.5) Power function

\[ \boxed{D_1(x^\alpha)=\alpha} \]

2.6) Chain rule (relative form)

Let \(u=u(x)>0\) and \(f=f(u)>0\). Then:

\[ \boxed{ D_1\big(f(u(x))\big)=\big(D_1 f\big)(u)\cdot D_1 u(x) } \]
Meaning: the relative rate of change of a composition equals the product of relative rates.

4) Generalization to \(D_n\)

In the hierarchical framework, each level corresponds to a scale \(\ln^{(n)}x\).

\[ \boxed{ D_n f(x)=\frac{d}{d\ln^{(n)}x}\big(\ln f(x)\big) } \]

The same structural rules extend across levels (under the same positivity/domain assumptions):

\[ \boxed{ D_n(fg)=D_n f + D_n g,\quad D_n\!\left(\frac{f}{g}\right)=D_n f - D_n g,\quad D_n(f^a)=a\,D_n f } \]
Hierarchical superposition: level 0 is additive, level 1 is multiplicative, higher levels follow the hierarchy of scales.
